A note from the creator
Why I built
this

Hi, I am a 10th grader from China, studying at The Master School. I started this project because cancer has been part of my family for as long as I can remember, and I needed somewhere to put that.

My mother was diagnosed with breast cancer last year. She is one of many — nearly every woman on her maternal side has faced cancer in some form. Watching her navigate that diagnosis from across an ocean taught me that cancer is never just a medical event. It ruptures everything.

My maternal grandfather passed away from leukemia before I was born — before any of my siblings were born. He never got to hold us. I have grown up knowing him only through stories, through the grief that never quite left my mother's family.

On my father's side, my grandfather, who is 91 years old, was recently diagnosed with liver cancer.

I built Through the Kaleidoscope because I believe awareness is not passive. It is the first act of change. I hope something you find here stays with you.

— Rose Yuan, Class of 2028

Through theKaleidoscope

MISH Community Service Fair  ·  April 18, 2026

A five-station gallery exploring cancer — its science, its human stories, its inequities, and what you can do about it.

"Cancer may touch your life — as a patient, family member, or friend. When it does, you'll be grateful you understood it from multiple perspectives. And you'll be angry that not everyone has the same fighting chance. That anger can fuel change."

The five stations — upstairs gallery
Station 01
The Mutiny Within

Cancer starts when DNA mutations cause cells to grow uncontrollably and evade the body's immune system. But it's not a death sentence — science has come a long way.

Flip the myth vs. fact cards, trace the mutation comic, and walk away knowing what cancer actually is — and isn't.

Myth vs. Fact flip cards Mutation comic strip True / false wall
Station 02
Walk in Their Scan

Cancer touches everyone differently. At this station you'll step into the perspectives of five people navigating cancer — a patient, a caregiver, a sibling, a nurse, and an insurance coordinator.

Listen to recorded stories and testimonials, including voices from Cancer Support Team patients and their families. Ask yourself: how would you show up for them?

Audio stories Written testimonials Persona cards
Station 03
The Zip Code Gap

Where you live should not determine whether you survive cancer. This station maps how cancer outcomes change by zip code — even within Westchester County.

Place your dot on the map, sort the barrier cards (housing, insurance, language, transportation, food access), and see how structural inequities shape who gets care.

Zip code mortality map Barrier card sort Disparity data wall
Station 04
Choose Your Words

What you say — and don't say — to someone with cancer matters more than you think. Practice the DOs and DON'Ts with real conversation guides provided by Cancer Support Team nurses.

Then write an anonymous "Open when…" letter to a real cancer patient. Letters will be delivered by Cancer Support Team. Inspired by The Aerin Project.

DOs & DON'Ts guide Letter writing Conversation practice
Station 05
The Ripple Starts Here

40% of all US cancers are linked to factors you can actually change — diet, activity, smoking, stress, alcohol, and sleep. This station connects your daily choices to your long-term health.

Spin the risk dial, ask the expert your real questions, and leave with a commitment card and CST resources you can actually use.

About Cancer Support Team (CST)

CST is a Westchester-based nonprofit providing free cancer support services. If you or someone you know has been diagnosed, these services are available at no cost.

In-Home Nursing
Registered nurses visit your home to explain diagnoses, review treatment, and provide nutritional guidance.
Symptom Control
Help managing nausea, fatigue, pain, and chemo side effects from trained oncology nurses.
Counseling & Support Groups
Individual counseling and peer support groups for patients and families.
Financial Assistance
Help navigating financial challenges that come with a cancer diagnosis.
Transportation
Rides to and from cancer treatment appointments for patients in need.
Case Management
Coordination of community services, insurance navigation, and care resources.
Chemo Comfort Kits
Free kits for chemo patients: anti-nausea teas, soft caps, oral care, and a wellness journal.
Educational Resources
Lifelines booklets, CST Tips, newsletters, and community partner referrals.
